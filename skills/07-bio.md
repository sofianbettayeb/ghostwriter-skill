# Skill: /bio — Bio Writer

## What this does

Builds or rewrites a bio using the WHO + WHAT + WHY framework.
Works for LinkedIn, website about pages, speaker bios, and social profiles.
Clear over clever, always.

---

## When to use

Run `/bio` when:
- You need a bio for a new platform or context
- Your current bio reads like a job description
- You want to attract a specific type of reader or client
- You've changed your focus and need to update how you present yourself

---

## The bio framework

### Three things every bio must answer

**WHO are you?**
Your name and primary role or category.
Not your job title. Your category.
Bad: "Senior Content Strategist at Acme Corp"
Good: "I help Webflow agencies get more organic traffic without paid ads"

**WHAT do you do?**
What you create, build, or help people with.
Be specific. Vague bios attract nobody.
Bad: "I write about marketing and entrepreneurship"
Good: "I build free SEO tools for Webflow designers and write about what I learn"

**WHY should they trust you (credibility)?**
One line that earns the right to be listened to.
Can be: years of experience, a specific outcome, a known project, a named audience.
Bad: "Passionate about helping businesses grow"
Good: "Founder of AI SEO Copilot — 18,000 active installs"

### The binary decision rule
Every bio should force a binary: either this is exactly for you, or it's not.
A bio that tries to appeal to everyone appeals to no one.
Specificity is what makes the right person follow, reach out, or buy.

---

## Context-specific rules

### LinkedIn bio
- Max 220 characters for the headline (the line under your name)
- The "About" section allows more — use it for the full WHO/WHAT/WHY
- End the About section with what you want readers to do next (follow, reach out, visit a link)

### Website About page
- More space = more room for personal story
- Use the Golden Intersection: answer the reader's question AND tell a personal story
- First paragraph: WHO/WHAT/WHY
- Second paragraph: the human behind the work
- End with a CTA

### Speaker / event bio
- Third person, past tense for credentials
- One sentence on what you speak about
- One sentence on why you're qualified
- One memorable specific (number, name, project)

### Twitter / short profile bio
- 160 characters max
- WHO + WHAT + one credibility signal
- Optional: a bit of personality or dry humor — but clarity first

---

## Questions to ask before writing

If the user hasn't provided enough context, ask:
1. What platform is this bio for?
2. Who do you most want to attract? (reader, client, collaborator, employer)
3. What's the one thing you want them to do after reading your bio?
4. What's your strongest credibility signal right now?

## Output format

```
BIO: [platform / context]

─────────────────────────────────────
SHORT VERSION (1–2 sentences):
[Punchy, clear, platform-appropriate]

FULL VERSION (3–5 sentences):
[WHO + WHAT + WHY + CTA]

─────────────────────────────────────
NOTES:
WHO: [clear / missing]
WHAT: [specific / vague]
CREDIBILITY: [named / implied / missing]
BINARY DECISION: [forces a choice / too broad]
SUGGESTED NEXT STEP: [e.g., test the short version as your LinkedIn headline]
```

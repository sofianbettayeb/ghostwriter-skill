# Skill: /headline — Headline Workshop

## What this does

Takes a topic, draft headline, or piece of content and builds headline options
using the 5-piece framework. Tests each for clarity vs. cleverness.
Returns ranked options with notes.

---

## When to use

Run `/headline` when:
- You have a topic but no headline yet
- Your current headline feels flat, vague, or too clever
- You want to test whether your headline answers WHO / WHAT / WHY
- You're about to publish and want a final check

---

## The 5 pieces of every headline

Every headline is built from some combination of these:

### 1. Number (optional but powerful)
Sets the container. Readers know what they're getting.
"7 reasons", "3 steps", "10 mistakes"
If no number: the piece covers one overarching idea. That's fine — just own it.

### 2. WHAT (required)
The topic. Without this, readers don't click.
Must be immediately clear. No clever substitutes.

### 3. WHO (optional — use for niche content)
Names the audience directly.
"For Webflow designers", "For first-time founders", "For marketers who hate jargon"
If targeting a general audience, leave it out.

### 4. FEEL (often missing)
How should the reader feel about this topic?
Urgency, curiosity, relief, validation, challenge.
A headline without FEEL is just a label.

### 5. PROMISE (required)
What does the reader get in exchange for their time?
Name the outcome. Be specific.
Weak: "7 ways to improve your SEO"
Strong: "7 ways to fix the indexing issues killing your Webflow traffic"

---

## The Curiosity Gap rule

A great headline tells the reader:
- The beginning (where they are now)
- The end (where they could be)
- But NOT the middle (they have to read to find out)

Test every headline: does it give away the answer? If yes, rewrite.

---

## Clear > Clever rule

If in doubt, be clearer, not more creative.
Clever headlines that confuse = no clicks.
Clear headlines that promise value = clicks.

Ask: "If someone saw this headline with no context, would they know immediately what it's about and whether it's for them?"

---

## 10 proven formats to draw from

1. **Big number**: "3,000 Webflow sites have this indexing problem. Here's the fix."
2. **Dollar/metric**: "$0 in paid traffic — how organic search drove our first 10,000 users"
3. **Credible source**: "What Google's documentation says about JavaScript SEO (and what it gets wrong)"
4. **This just happened**: "Google just changed how it crawls SPAs. Here's what it means for Webflow"
5. **Question / Answer**: "Why isn't your Webflow site ranking? It's probably this."
6. **Success story**: "How a one-person Webflow agency tripled organic traffic in 90 days"
7. **Things that don't belong together**: "What baking bread taught me about content strategy"
8. **For the industry**: "5 things every Webflow SEO consultant should audit before quoting a retainer"
9. **Topic within topic**: "AEO is changing SEO (and most Webflow sites aren't ready)"
10. **X things people don't do**: "7 indexing checks Webflow developers skip every time"

## Output format

```
HEADLINE WORKSHOP: [topic]

─────────────────────────────────────
OPTION 1 — [format name]
Headline: [headline]
WHO: [named / implied / general]
WHAT: [clear / unclear]
PROMISE: [specific / vague]
CURIOSITY GAP: [open / closed]
VERDICT: [one sentence — what works and what doesn't]

─────────────────────────────────────
OPTION 2 — [format name]
[same structure]

─────────────────────────────────────
OPTION 3 — [format name]
[same structure]

─────────────────────────────────────
RECOMMENDED: Option [X]
WHY: [one sentence]
```

Generate 3 options minimum. Max 5.
Always include at least one niche version and one general version.
Apply writing rules from `writing-rules.md`.

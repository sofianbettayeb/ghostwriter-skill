# Skill: /tequila — Content Differentiation

## What this does

Runs the Tequila Test on a topic or draft.
Identifies what's been said a hundred times already.
Pushes toward the angle that's actually different.

---

## When to use

Run `/tequila` when:
- You suspect your piece says the same thing as everyone else
- You're writing about a popular topic and want to stand out
- You have a draft that feels generic but you can't pinpoint why
- You're planning content and want to pre-check differentiation before writing

---

## The Tequila Test

### Step 1: List the obvious answers
For the given topic, generate every cliché, conventional wisdom, and common angle.
These are the things 80% of writers would say.
Be exhaustive. Include the boring, the safe, and the recycled.

### Step 2: Discard the list
Everything on that list is off the table.
That's what everyone else is writing.

### Step 3: Find the tequila
What's the unexpected angle?
What would make a reader stop and say "I haven't seen that before"?

The tequila angle is usually:
- A contrarian take on conventional wisdom
- A specific personal experience that disproves the common advice
- A data point or case study nobody's talking about
- The thing everyone knows but nobody says out loud
- A reframe that changes how the reader sees the whole topic

### Step 4: Apply the Magic Triangle
Three levers for differentiation. Use at least one:

- **Voice**: Your specific perspective, history, or personality. Nobody can copy your exact experience.
- **Format**: Present the same idea in an unexpected structure. A failure story instead of a success story. A list of what NOT to do instead of what to do.
- **Content**: Say something that hasn't been said. Find the gap in the conversation.

---

## Output format

```
TEQUILA TEST: [topic]

─────────────────────────────────────
WHAT EVERYONE ELSE IS SAYING:
[Bullet list of 5–8 clichéd angles on this topic]

─────────────────────────────────────
THE GAP:
[One paragraph — what's missing from the conversation on this topic]

─────────────────────────────────────
YOUR TEQUILA ANGLES:

Option A — Voice differentiation:
[Angle that draws on your specific experience or perspective]

Option B — Format differentiation:
[Angle that flips the expected structure]

Option C — Content differentiation:
[Angle that says something that hasn't been said]

─────────────────────────────────────
RECOMMENDED: Option [X]
WHY: [One sentence — why this angle is most defensible for this author]
```

After output, ask: "Want to develop the recommended angle into a full outline, or run `/headline` on it first?"

Apply writing rules from `writing-rules.md`.
